About NCICT

National Cancer Institute dosimetry system for Computed Tomography (NCICT) is a computational solution to estimate organ doses for pediatric and adult patients undergoing Computed Tomography (CT) scans. The program provides absorbed dose to major radiosensitive organs and tissues based on the characteristics of patients and CT scan parameters. NCICT is based on several key technologies that previously published including computational human phantoms (Lee et al 2007, 2008, Hurtado et al 2012, Lee et al 2010), the simulation of reference CT scanner (Lee et al 2011), the experimental validations of simulated organ dose (Long et al 2013), the library of normalized and weighted CTDI (Lee et al 2014), and the compilation of comprehensive dose conversion coefficients (Lee et al 2011, 2012, 2015).
